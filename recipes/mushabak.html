mushabak

Description

Mushabak (or Mushebek) is the other iconic crown jewel of the Dire Dawa and Harar sweet scenes. Standing side-by-side with Halawa in eastern Ethiopian markets, Mushabak is a vibrant, deep-fried funnel cake or spiral sweet that is incredibly crunchy on the outside, hollow and juicy on the inside, and drenched in aromatic sugar syrup

Ingrideants

  • 1 cup White sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 tbsp Lemon juice (prevents crystallization)
  • 5 to 7 Green cardamom pods (crushed)
  • 1 small Cinnamon stick piece

Steps

  1. Simmer the Spiced Syrup First
    In a small saucepan, combine the sugar, water, lemon juice, crushed cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then simmer gently for 5–7 minutes until it slightly thickens into a glossy, sticky syrup. Turn off the heat and let it cool down; it needs to be warm or room temperature when the hot pastry hits it.
  2. Whisk the Mushabak Batter
    In a deep bowl, mix the all-purpose flour and baking powder. Whisk in the plain yogurt, food coloring, and slowly pour in the warm water. Whisk vigorously until a completely smooth, silk-like batter forms. It should be pourable but thick—similar to heavy cream or pancake batter. Let the batter rest for 15–20 minutes to allow the flour to absorb the liquid.
  3. Fill Your Piping Setup
    Pour the batter into a pastry bag fitted with a small round tip, a clean plastic squeeze bottle (like a ketchup bottle), or a ziplock bag with a tiny corner nipped off.
  4. Fry in Hot Spirals
    Heat 1 to 2 inches of vegetable oil in a wide, flat skillet over medium-high heat. Test the heat with a drop of batter; it should immediately pop to the surface sizzling. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil, starting from the center and swirling outward in a concentric circle to form a lacy round disc (about the size of your palm).
  5. Fry Until Ultra-Crispy
    Let the Mushabak fry for roughly 2 minutes on the first side until it holds its structural shape and turns hard. Carefully flip it with tongs or a slotted spoon and fry the other side for another 1–2 minutes. It should feel rigidly crisp to the touch
  6. Dunk, Soak, and DrainLift the hot Mushabak out of the oil, shake off the excess grease, and plunge it immediately into the warm spiced sugar syrup. Let it submerge and drink up the syrup for about 30 to 45 seconds. Remove it and let it rest on a wire rack or mesh strainer to shed any dripping syrup. Repeat for the remaining batter

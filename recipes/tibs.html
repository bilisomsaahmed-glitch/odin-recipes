Tibs

Description

Ethiopian Tibs is a classic, rapid-fire stir-fry traditionally made with beef or lamb, seared on blistering heat and coated in rich, aromatic spices. Unlike long-simmered African stews, this popular comfort food comes together in roughly 20 to 30 minutes once prepped.This authentic recipe details how to make Awaze Tibs, featuring juicy cubed beef tossed with the iconic berbere spice blend, herbed niter kibbeh, and fresh aromatics

Ingrideants

  • 1.5 lbs beef tenderloin, ribeye, or sirloin (cut into 1-inch cubes)
  • 3 tbsp niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced clarified butter—or substitute plain ghee)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable or olive oil (divided)
  • 1 large red onion (sliced into thin strips, not diced)
  • 1 to 2 jalapeño or serrano peppers (sliced)
  • 2 Roma tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Steps

  1. Mix the Awaze Paste
    a small bowl, whisk together the berbere spice blend, minced garlic, grated ginger, and warm water (or wine) until a smooth, thick paste forms. Set aside
  2. Sear the Beef Hot and Fast
    Pat the cubed beef completely dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and black pepper. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a heavy cast-iron skillet or wok over high heat until lightly smoking. Add the meat in a single layer (work in batches if your pan is small). Sear undisturbed for 2–3 minutes until a dark, rich crust forms, then flip and brown the other side for 1–2 minutes. The inside should still be rare. Remove the beef and its juices to a plate.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics
    Lower the heat slightly to medium-high. Add the remaining oil and the niter kibbeh (spiced butter) to the same skillet. Toss in the sliced red onions and a few pieces of jalapeño. Cook for 3–4 minutes until the onions soften and pick up the browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  4. Bloom the Spices and TomatoesPour the prepared Awaze spice paste directly into the onions and stir rapidly for about 1 minute to cook out the raw spice flavor. Add the chopped tomatoes and the rosemary sprig. Simmer for 2–3 minutes until the tomatoes begin to collapse and form a rich, thick gravy.
  5. Combine and Finish
    Return the seared beef and any pooled plate juices back into the skillet. Toss everything together for 2 minutes so the meat absorbs the fiery sauce. Throw in the remaining fresh jalapeño slices for crunch and color. Pour in the lemon juice, remove the rosemary sprig, and pull the skillet off the heat.

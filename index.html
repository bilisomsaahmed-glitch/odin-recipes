Ethiopian cuisine features a dynamic balance of savory and sweet flavors, beautifully showcased by combining ⁠Tibs with eastern region desserts like Mushabak and Halawa. ⁠Tibs is a foundational, savory dish made of pan-seared beef or lamb sautéed with spiced butter (niter kibbeh), rosemary, and peppers. In contrast, Mushabak (or Mushebek) is a popular spiral-shaped, deep-fried pastry soaked in aromatic sugar syrup, famous in cities like Dire Dawa. Halawa (or Halwa) rounds out the sweet side as a rich, dense confection often crafted from ingredients like tahini, sugar, or blended fruits. Together, these dishes highlight the diverse regional culinary influences found across Ethiopia
